Google Wallet

Brainstorming

Overview

Skills: Ideation, Brainstorming

In the Google Design Jam, held in our school in January 2014, we were given the task of generating ideas to increase the brand awareness of Google Wallet as well as relieving people's safty worries. We, a team of 5 students did brainstorming and ideation for 1.5 hours and came out with good ideas for recommendation.

Google Wallet
Google Wallet is digital money payment system which allows to send or receive money, redeem Google offers at stores, to do online payment and to withdraw money from ATMs.

Goals
Create Brand Awareness, Constraints: Enhance User Trust.

Focus
To leverage the most used existing Google products- Google Search Engine, Chrome, Gmail. To create analogy experience of having a wallet with the user.

Security Idea
Face recognition, Fingerprint scan, Voice recognition can be used as a paying mode or additional security layer. It can also be used in the cases when the users forget or lose their wallet, cards, or when the phone's battery is down.

compatibility Problem

Idea One

Now: Google wallet is supported only on gmail.com domain but not on other domains like umich.edu. Most used: students, IT employees, etc. Can be added: Other domains should support it.

Gmail Widget

Idea Two

Gmail: Recommend Google wallet by detecting keywords related to money, currency or numerical data.
Ex. “You can now send money using Google Wallet”

Search Engine

Idea Three

Google search catches user’s attention by Google Doodle games and play icon. Embed Graphical Wallet in Doodle.
Google Search Engine: Showing Google Wallet icon when users attempt paying online. Ex. “You can pay online with Google Wallet”
Google Search Engine: Adding a video like a game on Google Doodle.

Payment Method

Idea Four

Pay with Google icon. Incorporating in Bank cards. Logo of Google Wallet in “shop on Google”. Small monthly payments option.

BOSCH ATV

BOSCH ATV

ATV Panel

Infotainment Cluster For Side-By-Side Vehicle

Skills: Brainstorming, Ideation, Sketching

Overview
In the BOSCH Design Jam, held in our school in April 2014, we (Team of three) were given the task of generating ideas to design interactive ATV system. Background
The powersports market lags far behind automotive when it comes to in-vehicle infotainment options. Riders are turning to expensive aftermarket solutions for their vehicles that are usually not optimized for the unique conditions to offroading.

Challenges
Design the layout for a color, digital display cluster that will be integrated into a Side-by-Side Powersports Vehicle. The features to include on the display are as follows:
- Viewing real time vehicle data (speed, RPM, etc.)
- Viewing stability control and G-Force information
- On and offroad navigation capabilities
- Go Pro Video Integration
- Weather alerts
- Audio Integration
- Fleet Management
- Smartphone connectivity (checking text messages, phone reception)

What we consider in 150 minutes
1. How will the driver be able to use these features – when stopped or in park, and when driving? Should any functionality be changed or disabled while the vehicle is in motion?
2. How should this technology be compatible with the user’s smartphone? Do any functions require a smartphone to work (GPS, Audio, etc.)?
3. How will your design be unique to a powersports vehicle vs. a normal car? For example, driving a side-by-side may be much louder than driving a car. How can listening to music or talking on the phone be realized in this type of environment? What other experiences are unique to powersports vehicles?
4. How will your design showcase the most important features to a customer? What will make them choose a vehicle with this display over a normal one?
5. Are there other functions not listed which could improve the rider’s experience? How could these be implemented along with the existing requirements?

Seelio Design Jam

Empower Seelio

Skills: Brainstorming, Ideation, Sketching

Overview
Seelio is an edtech company that helps students capture their story in online portfolios so they're career ready. In the Design Jam, held in our school in November 2014, we (team of four) were given the task of generating ideas to improve usage and user experience.

Challenges
- Many segmentations. Users use Seelio from start to finish of a student's career. Admissions uses it to attract students; freshmen incorporate it in their first semester; Career Services uses it to help put students and employers together; and by graduation, we can track the growth of every student.
- Limited users so far. Need to reate a strong brand for prospective students. Seelio should be special.

What we consider in 90 minutes
1. To empower students in telling their professional stories to employers.
2. Create a special area for prospective job-seeking students.

Our solutions
1. Interactions with hiring companies.
What if company release Problem Challenges on Seelio? Some companies prefer to give out challenges books to potential applicants in order to gather more information from them and judge their suitability. Seelio can generate Challenging Sections for hiring companies assissting them receive more information. The top left picture shows one sample challenge on Google Wallet. Write-up structure is given in order to let challengers narrow down on the right path and think deep. Seelio users can claim the challenges. After submitting, companies will go through the answers and give interviews to the people they are interested in. Videos, pictures and other artifacts can be added.
This is kind of a win-win idea. Students can know more about the company, the produc when practicing skills. Companies can get more efficient and target information.
Seelio Challenges can not only display directly on Seelio, but also can be embedded into companies job applications. As the second left pictures shows.

2. One more showing platform
Users can choose to publish their past challenges publicly. There will be an specific area for displaying company challenges. Recruiters can go through these challenges to judge skills and abilities and find their matching ones. The left bottom picture shows the platform. The right bottom picture shows the panel for students seeing their submiited or on going challenges.
