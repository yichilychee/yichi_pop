A.C.E. Care Management

Our team

Team Statement

A.C.E. Care Management is a SaaS business, currently developing a care management software for clinical providers of elder care. We believe that Accountability, Continuity of Information, and Efficiency are keys to increasing care quality, reducing hospital readmissions, and improving and patient outcomes. The A.C.E solution is a tool that supports the transition of post-discharge care expectations from hospital to elder care facilities and allows for the monitoring of compliance with that plan.

Our team is attending two challenges, one is IIA, the other is MBC. Updates of product and competition will come soon.

Innovation in Action

Skills: Interview, Analysis, Prototype, Presenting

The Innovation in Action: Solutions to Public Health Challenges competition harnesses the talents of Michigan students to address public health problems. Within this framework, the program equips students with an innovator's toolkit in a supportive environment where they combine these skills with their creativity and passion to impact the public's health.
Through the course of this competitive 6-month, transdisciplinary experience, you and your team will:
* Invent, design, and steward your innovation.
* Meet new people, encounter new concepts, and develop a greater understanding of public health needs.
* Hone your innovation and entrepreneurial skills through modules—the program's experiential learning framework.
* Fully develop your idea and be prepared to pitch to a panel of experts in innovation.
* Compete for cash prizes.

Michigan Business Challenge

Skills: Entrepreneurship

The Michigan Business Challenge is a campus-wide, multi-round business plan competition where student teams have the opportunity to win cash prizes totaling over $75,000, gain feedback from judges and expand their business network. In addition to cash prizes, the winning team members will each receive a Fitbit package which includes a Fitbit Tracker, a Fitbit Aria Wifi Scale, and Fitbit Gear.
The multi-round Michigan Business Challenge takes participants through the entire cycle of new venture creation from creating a strong pitch to conducting a marketing and financial assessment and writing a comprehensive business plan. The competition kicks-off in December with more than 80 teams competing in Round 1 and concluding in February with four competing teams at the finals. The Zell Lurie Institute facilitates start-up workshops to help prepare student teams for the deliverables due for each round of the competition.
Now we successfully passed Round One and are waiting for the result from Round Two.
