Recipes of your favorite restaurant

Abstract

Skills: Data manipulation and visualization, Python

Recently, daily and useful data analysis and visualization attract more and more attention. This data manipulation project can help people getting the recipes from their favorite restaurant as well as knowing what kind of ingredients most likely been used in various type of restaurant. The specific goal I try to address is let the product of this project to find out the corresponding recipes to user’s restaurant choice.

Targets

my target audience is the entry-level group of people who have great interests in data analysis. I want to introduce my experience to those who start accomplishing tasks including gathering the data, parsing it, and summarizing it in a quick and easy, useful and repeatable way.

Audience

I used the Yelp's Academic Dataset. I got its information from its website: https://www.yelp.com/academic_dataset. The real dataset I used for coding is from the instructor’s offer in homework 2. It is a JSON format dataset. I downloaded it from the MBox provided by instructor. There are 474434 records in the JSON file. The dataset has three objects of data. I only use two objects of the dataset, review and business. Review type of data has 330071 records and business type of data has 13490 records.

Introduction

People do not always have the chance to eat in their favorite restaurant and someone may enjoy cooking the delicious dish himself or herself. My data manipulation project can solve the above problems. The specific goal I try to address is let the tool to find out the corresponding recipes to user’s restaurant choice. For example, if one searches Sadako Japanese Restaurant in Ann Arbor, it will show your popular recipes about Sadako’s highlights. Then the user can quickly follow the recipe to make a wonderful dinner.

Data Source

The two data sources I used are Yelp Review Search API and BigOven Recipe API. Data format in Yelp API is JSON while in BigOven is XML. Default Rate (Beta) is 100 GET Requests / Hour.
